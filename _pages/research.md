## Research Overview

My research sits at the intersection of atmospheric chemistry, climate science, and Earth system modelling, with a strong interest in the processes that shape cloud formation and their role in the climate system.

## Current Research

### Ice-Nucleating Particles in Mixed-Phase Clouds

As a postdoctoral scientist at ETlsH Zurich, I am investigating the role of ice-nucleating particles in mixed-phase clouds using global climate models. This work focuses on understanding how these particles influence cloud microphysics and their broader implications for climate.

As a fellow at the European Space Agency (ESA) in Frascati, Italy, I worked on Earth observation projects, gaining valuable experience in remote sensing applications for atmospheric and climate research.

## Research Interests

- **Atmospheric Chemistry**: Understanding chemical processes in the atmosphere and their climate implications
- **Cloud Microphysics**: Investigating the formation and evolution of clouds, particularly mixed-phase clouds
- **Earth System Modelling**: Using numerical models to study interactions between different components of the Earth system
- **Biogenic Emissions**: Studying how vegetation influences atmospheric composition through volatile organic compounds
- **Climate Science**: Examining the role of various atmospheric processes in the climate system

## Research Methods

- Global climate modeling
- Atmospheric chemistry simulations
- Earth system modeling
- Remote sensing and satellite data analysis
- Field measurements and laboratory studies
